About

This web archive juxtaposes images of the countercultural collective, Vocations for Social Change, with contemporary Google Maps searches of the addresses found in their directory. The collective once relied on the directory to organize their close-knit community and disseminate their ideology. When the directory fell out of use, so did the collective, marking the decline of their movement. By pairing historical imagery with current digital representations of these locations, the archive reflects the physical and societal changes brought about by the loss of the directory. It prompts us to consider not only how digital platforms have redefined access and privacy but also the tangible consequences for spaces once defined by collective action. This project highlights what we lose and what we gain as we transition from analog to digital networks.
